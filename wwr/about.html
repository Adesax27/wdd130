White Waters Rafters

Our Mission

Rafting is an exhilarating outdoor adventure activity that involves navigating a river, often through "whitewater" rapids, using a durable inflatable raft and paddles. It is a popular team sport that requires coordination, communication, and, most importantly, teamwork among a group of 4 to 12 people to successfully navigate the water.

History

Rafting's history evolved from ancient log rafts for transport to a modern inflatable sport, with the first recorded attempt on the U.S. Snake River in 1811, the invention of the rubber raft by Lt. John Fremont in the 1840s, and its rise as a commercial sport in the 1940s, gaining international recognition after the 1972 Munich Olympics.
