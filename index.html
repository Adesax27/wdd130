The Abidjan Ivory Coast Temple, the first for the nation, was announced in 2015 by President Thomas S. Monson and dedicated on May 25, 2025, by Elder Ronald A. Rasband of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, serving rapidly growing local members who previously traveled to Ghana. Construction began with a 2018 groundbreaking led by Elder Neil L. Andersen, featuring Mid-Century Modern design with African elements, and concluded with a public open house in May 2025 before its dedication, symbolizing a spiritual oasis in West Africa.
